Information Overload 2011-01-09

Jan 9th, 2011

  • Transcending the Human, DIY Style
    DIY biohacking and transhumanism. I have a feeling we’ll see more of this in the not too distant future.
  • Six ads that changed the way you think
    I’ve always been interested in good ads and this BBC article lists some that each changed the industry in their own way.
  • The surprising usefulness of sloppy arithmetic
    A computer chip providing imprecise answers could speed up tasks like video processing or human-computer interaction.
  • Everything the Internet Knows About Me
    “Lifelogging” can indeed be quite interesting, which is why I use sites like Foursquare (but often don’t share my checkins etc.).
  • Clickable Index Map of Mathematics
    While not exactly visually pleasing, this is an interesting way to browse through the body of modern mathematics.
  • Game on for the virtual sweatshop
    The Financial Times on WoW, gold farming and digital economies.
  • The Agonizing Last Words of Programmer Bill Zeller
    Bill Zeller’s suicide note, a talented individual who took his own life because he was sexually abused as a child.
  • The subject of suicide & why I’m alive today
    Mashable journalist Jolie O’Dell tells the story of her own attempted suicide as a reaction to Bill Zeller’s.
  • Francis Ford Coppola: On Risk, Money, Craft & Collaboration
    Very nice Q&A session with Francis Ford Coppola.
  • “Ich bin ein weisser Neger”
    Very interesting interview with author, former professor of sociology and UN Special Rapporteur on the Right to Food, Jean Ziegler.
