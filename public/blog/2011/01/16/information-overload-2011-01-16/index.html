Information Overload 2011-01-16

Jan 16th, 2011

  • Crap Detection 101
    The next time you want to help improve someone’s information literacy, point them to this article. It may even have some interesting links for more senior sceptics.
  • Vanessa Hurst, Destroying the Female Programmer Stereotype
    There should be more articles like this to close the gender gap in IT.
  • Shades of Grey
    Another text asking or a more differentiated discussion of the whole Wikileaks situation. I couldn’t agree more.
  • Programming is not a craft
    Since I’m a big fan of balanced opinions, here’s an interesting contribution to the software craftsmanship discussion, as well as an answer to that article.
  • Inequality in Equalland
    To stay with the topic of this issue here’s more food for though, this time on economic inequality.
  • Your Random Numbers – Getting Started with Processing and Data Visualization
    With so much data out there, why not start visualizing it yourself. Processing is a neat tool for that and this tutorial gives a first taste.
  • THE ZIMBABWEAN Trillion Dollar
    Awesome campaign by newspaper The Zimbabwean
  • Man of Mistery
    Interesting article on the success of Stieg Larsson’s “Millennium Trilogy”. Contains lots of spoilers, so don’t read this if you still want to read the books.
  • The Mathematics Of Beauty
    Entertaining post on the OkCupid blog about male messaging behavior in regard to women’s attractiveness.
  • Why Physicians Don’t Cry
    Former colleague and M.D. Lukas published this in his new online magazine MedCrunch.
