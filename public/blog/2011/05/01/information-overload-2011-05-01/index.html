Information Overload 2011-05-01

May 1st, 2011

  • Solar power goes viral
    A genetically modified virus increases the efficiency of solar cells.
  • Digging Deeper
    In a time where it seems like a lot of journalists just seem to copy whatever they find online, it’s refreshing when a journalist from a small newspaper – who above all is a science and data geek – wins a Pulitzer Price.
  • Guantanamo leaks lift lid on world’s most controversial prison
    The Guardian covers the Guantanamo leaks, Including a video interview with Clive Stafford Smith, legal director of Reprieve, as well as an interactive presentation of all the prisoners’ files.
  • Eating Healthily for $3 a Day
    Bad eating habits are quite common in the hacker community, so I decided to include this link. For me the main problem when it comes to healthy eating is having to work, but even there you can manage without too much overhead.
  • Richard Feynman on education in Brazil
    A good read. During my academic life I met enough people who considered memorizing more important than understanding.
  • The Consequentialist
    A very long and detailed article by the New Yorker that helps better understand the Obama administration’s foreign policy.
  • Amazon’s $23,698,655.93 book about flies
    On the wonders of algorithmic pricing.
  • LSD: The Geek’s Wonder Drug?
    This article is already 5 years old and I feel we still haven’t moved forward in rehabilitating certain drugs and using them for good.
  • The Ten Commandments of Egoless Programming
    Another article from 2006, but still as true today as it was back then.
  • Programming may one day be about getting the maths right
    Yes, I’m an FP aficionado, so what? ;-)
